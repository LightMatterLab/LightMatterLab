About Us

Our group is primarily focused on light-matter interactions in the mesoscopic domainâ€”where light interacts with micro or nano particles. We employ 'optical tweezers,' where light is focused to sub-micron spot sizes to confine and manipulate single mesoscopic particles, to study light-matter interactions in the regime of classical physics. We have four inverted microscopes: Zeiss (Axiovert.A1), Olympus (IX71), Thorlabs (OTM), and Thorlabs (OTKB).

Research

Our research areas include:

  • Spin-Orbit Interaction: Investigating the coupling between the spin and orbital angular momentum of light in tightly focused beams.
  • Soft Matter Physics: Studying the behavior of soft materials under optical manipulation.
  • Physics of Micro-bubbles: Exploring the dynamics and applications of micro-bubbles generated using laser-induced methods.
  • Biophotonics: Applying optical techniques to study biological systems at the microscopic level.
  • Air Trap: Developing methods to trap and manipulate particles in air using optical forces.
